Most Suppliers Can't Prove What They're Selling

After sourcing peptides for a health performance business with the highest possible standards, we spent eight months and thousands of outreach attempts — VA teams working four hours a day each — just to find suppliers who could actually back their claims with documentation.

Every supplier promised a Certificate of Analysis. Almost none could deliver one that was genuine, complete, and QR-verified. If most manufacturers aren't even running third-party testing, it means they genuinely don't know what they're selling — and neither do you.

No COA = No Accountability

A supplier without a Certificate of Analysis has no independent proof their product is what they claim it is. They simply don't deserve a place in the market.

Cropped QR Codes = Borrowed Certificates

Every genuine Janoshik COA has a QR code linking to the live certificate. A missing or cropped QR code is a red flag that the document has been repurposed from another batch.

Many suppliers only test for purity. But sterility failures, endotoxin contamination, and heavy metals are invisible to a purity HPLC — and they're the real safety risks.

A Certificate of Analysis is a document issued by an independent third-party laboratory confirming the identity, purity, and quality of a compound. It is the fundamental proof that a product has been tested and meets stated specifications. Without a COA, you have only the supplier's word — and that isn't good enough for research-grade supply.
Every COA we provide is issued by Janoshik, the industry's most recognised peptide testing laboratory. Each certificate includes a unique QR code at the bottom that links directly to the live certificate on Janoshik's website. Simply scan the QR code and you'll see the original certificate — confirming it was genuinely issued for our specific batch, not copied from another source. If a supplier's COA doesn't have a QR code, or the QR code has been cropped off, treat that as a serious red flag.
