The Industry Problem

Most Suppliers Can't Prove What They're Selling

We built Prime Manufacturing after spending eight months and thousands of supplier contacts — two full-time VAs working four hours a day each — just to find sources that could back their claims with genuine, verifiable documentation.

Every supplier promised a Certificate of Analysis. Almost none could deliver one that was authentic, complete, and QR-verified by Janoshik. If a manufacturer isn't running third-party testing, they don't know what they're producing — and neither does the buyer.

"If a company chops off the QR code on the COA, they're using someone else's certificate. They either know they don't have good stuff, or they're cutting costs. Either way, they don't deserve a place in the market."

— The lesson from 8 months of vetting thousands of suppliers
✗

No COA = No Accountability

A supplier without a Certificate of Analysis has zero independent proof their product is what they claim. Full stop.

⚠

Cropped QR = Borrowed Certificate

Genuine Janoshik COAs include a QR code linking to the live certificate. No QR code — or a cropped one — means the document was taken from another supplier's batch.

⚗

Purity Testing Alone Isn't Enough

HPLC purity won't detect microbial contamination, endotoxins, or heavy metals. These are the risks most suppliers skip testing for entirely.

✓

Prime Tests All Five. Every Batch.

Purity, identity, sterility, endotoxins, and heavy metals — documented and QR-verified by Janoshik on every order we ship.

A Certificate of Analysis is a document from an independent third-party laboratory confirming the identity, purity, and safety of a compound. Without a COA, you only have the supplier's word that their product is what they claim — and in the peptide industry, that is not good enough. Our COAs are issued by Janoshik, the most recognised peptide testing laboratory globally.
Every Janoshik COA we provide includes a unique QR code at the bottom that links directly to the live certificate on Janoshik's website. Scan it and you'll see the original certificate — confirming it was issued for our specific batch. If a supplier's COA is missing its QR code, or the code has been cropped out, treat that as a serious red flag: it almost certainly means the certificate belongs to a different batch or supplier.
