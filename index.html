FAQ

Common questions

A Certificate of Analysis is a document from an independent third-party laboratory confirming the identity, purity, and safety of a compound. It is the fundamental proof that a product has been tested and meets its stated specifications. Without a COA from an independent lab, you have only the manufacturer's word — which carries no external accountability. Our COAs are issued by Janoshik, the most recognised peptide testing laboratory globally, and every certificate includes a live QR code linking directly to the result on their website.
Every Janoshik COA includes a unique QR code that links to the live, unedited certificate on Janoshik's own website. Scan it and you'll confirm the batch reference and all results match. If a supplier's COA is missing a QR code — or it's been cropped — treat that as a serious red flag. It almost certainly means the certificate belongs to a different batch or supplier. Every certificate we supply has a full, scannable QR code. We encourage you to verify every one.
Five tests: HPLC purity (≥99% standard), identity by mass spectrometry, microbial sterility, endotoxin via LAL assay, and a full heavy metals panel via ICP-MS. All five are reported on the Janoshik COA that ships with your order. Most suppliers only test for purity — the remaining four are what separate a genuinely characterised compound from one that merely looks good on paper.
